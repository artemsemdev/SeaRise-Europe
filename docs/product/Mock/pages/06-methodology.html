How to Interpret
Your Results

SeaRise Europe combines authoritative climate projections, high-resolution elevation data, and transparent methodology to produce scenario-based coastal exposure assessments. This page explains what the results mean, how they are generated, and what they do not cover.

1 What We Show

SeaRise Europe displays scenario-based coastal sea-level exposure for European locations. When you search for a location, the system determines whether it falls within a coastal analysis zone and, if so, calculates projected exposure under the selected climate scenario and time horizon.

Results include a projected sea-level rise value, an uncertainty range, and a visual overlay on the map showing areas that may be exposed under the selected conditions.

2 Data Sources

All projections are derived from peer-reviewed, publicly available datasets:

Projections IPCC AR6 Sea Level Projections Regional sea-level rise projections under Shared Socioeconomic Pathways (SSPs). Provides median values and confidence ranges for multiple time horizons through 2150.
Elevation Copernicus DEM (GLO-30) Global 30-meter resolution digital elevation model derived from radar satellite imagery. Used to determine terrain elevation relative to current sea level.
Indicator EEA Extreme Sea Levels Indicator European Environment Agency indicator on extreme sea levels and coastal flooding, providing context for the frequency and impact of sea-level events along European coastlines.
Geocoding Nominatim (OpenStreetMap) Free-text location search and geocoding service used to resolve user queries into geographic coordinates within the European coverage area.

3 Climate Scenarios

SeaRise Europe supports projections under three Shared Socioeconomic Pathways (SSPs) from the IPCC Sixth Assessment Report:

  • SSP1-1.9 — Very low greenhouse gas emissions scenario. Assumes rapid and deep emissions reductions, reaching net-zero CO₂ around 2050. Represents the most optimistic pathway.
  • SSP2-4.5 — Intermediate scenario. Assumes moderate mitigation efforts with emissions peaking around mid-century. Often used as a "middle of the road" reference.
  • SSP5-8.5 — Very high emissions scenario. Assumes continued fossil fuel reliance with minimal climate policy. Represents the upper boundary of projected change.

4 Time Horizons

Results are available for three future time horizons:

  • 2030 — Near-term horizon. Differences between scenarios are relatively small at this range, as committed warming dominates.
  • 2050 — Mid-century horizon. Scenario divergence becomes more visible, especially between SSP1-1.9 and SSP5-8.5.
  • 2100 — End-of-century horizon. Maximum divergence between pathways. Uncertainty ranges are widest at this horizon.

5 Interpretation Guidance

All results should be understood as modeled projections under specified conditions, not predictions or forecasts. Key points:

  • Projected values represent the median estimate from the AR6 ensemble for the selected scenario and horizon, with a stated uncertainty range.
  • The exposure overlay shows areas where terrain elevation is at or below the projected sea-level rise value. It does not account for local flood defenses, drainage systems, or land-use changes.
  • Infrastructure impact counts are modeled estimates based on geospatial datasets and should not be interpreted as confirmed counts.
  • Results update when you change the scenario, time horizon, or location — they always reflect the currently active selection.

6 Key Limitations

Not a legal, engineering, insurance, or financial determination. Results are for informational and educational purposes only. Do not use this tool to make property, investment, or safety decisions without consulting qualified professionals.
  • Elevation data (30m resolution) is not detailed enough for parcel-level or building-level assessments.
  • The model does not include storm surge, tidal extremes, wave action, subsidence, or glacial isostatic adjustment at the local level.
  • Inland flood risk (rivers, rainfall, groundwater) is not assessed in this version.
  • Results are based on the current coastline and do not model future adaptation measures or land reclamation.
  • Data availability may vary by region. Some coastal areas may return "Data Unavailable" if the required datasets do not cover the location.
